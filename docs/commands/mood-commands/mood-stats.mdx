# /mood stats

View your mood statistics as insightful percentages and associations. Use this command to see trends in your emotional check-ins—including your most common moods, activity pairings, and summary statements (e.g., “When feeling grateful you also logged hobbies (2 times)”). Stats are based on where you logged moods: server stats only count logs made in that server, and DMs are tracked separately.

<hr className="md-divider-gradient" />

<h3 className="heading-with-icon">
  <img src="/img/icons/chat.svg" alt="" className="heading-icon" />
  <span>Usage</span>
</h3>
```bash
/mood stats [period]
```
- `[period]` – (Optional) Specify a time period for your stats (e.g., week, month, all). If omitted, shows stats for all time.

:::secondary
**This command is specifically for seeing your mood statistics over a period of time.**
:::

<hr className="md-divider-gradient" />

<h3 className="heading-with-icon">
  <img src="/img/icons/example.svg" alt="" className="heading-icon" />
  <span>Example</span>
</h3>
```bash
/mood stats month
```
This will:
- Display an embed summarizing your mood logs for the past month
- Show a percentage breakdown of your most frequently selected moods
- List the activities you most often paired with each mood
- Include summary statements like: “When feeling grateful you also logged hobbies (2 times)”

<hr className="md-divider-gradient" />

<h3 className="heading-with-icon">
  <img src="/img/icons/notes.svg" alt="" className="heading-icon" />
  <span>Notes</span>
</h3>
> - Stats are tracked separately for each server and for in DMs
> - If you logged moods in DMs and run this command in a server, you’ll see zero stats for that server
> - Percentages help you spot mood and activity trends over your chosen time period
> - Use this to reflect on your wellbeing patterns and find what lifts your mood
