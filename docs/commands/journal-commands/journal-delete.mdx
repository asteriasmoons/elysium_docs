# /journal delete 

Delete one of your journal entries by its number. Use this command to permanently remove a specific entry from your private journal—helpful for cleaning up, correcting mistakes, or maintaining privacy.

<hr className="md-divider-gradient" />

<h3 className="heading-with-icon">
  <img src="/img/icons/chat.svg" alt="" className="heading-icon" />
  <span>Usage</span>
</h3>
```bash
/journal delete <index/number>
```
- `<index/number>` – The number of the journal entry you want to delete (as shown in your journal list)

:::warning
**Be certain of your choice to delete a journal entry because its irreversible.**
:::

<hr className="md-divider-gradient" />

<h3 className="heading-with-icon">
  <img src="/img/icons/example.svg" alt="" className="heading-icon" />
  <span>Example</span>
</h3>
```bash
/journal delete 3
```
This will:
- Permanently remove your third journal entry from your private journal
- Confirm the deletion with a message or embed
- Renumber your remaining entries automatically for consistency

<hr className="md-divider-gradient" />

<h3 className="heading-with-icon">
  <img src="/img/icons/notes.svg" alt="" className="heading-icon" />
  <span>Notes</span>
</h3>
> - You can only delete entries from your own journal
> - Deleted entries cannot be recovered—double-check before confirming
> - Use `/journal list` to see entry numbers and details before deleting
> - Entry numbers will be adjusted automatically after deletion

<h3 className="heading-with-icon">

  <span>Related Commands</span>
